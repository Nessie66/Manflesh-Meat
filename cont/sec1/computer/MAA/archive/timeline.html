Monogastric Timeline

5 billion years ago

The hills are very different... there is no town, there aren't even hills yet.

1-2 billion years ago

At some point in this time range it's believed the hills themselves were formed, either through forces beyond our comprehension, or just some geographical tectonic stuff or whatever! Boring!

400 million years ago

Apparently the tectonic plates smashed into each other, this probably had an effect on the world and therefore the hills but it's beyond the scope of this fake website.

300 million years ago

Speleogenesis occurs and the karst caves beneath the hills begin to develop. The rising and falling of the water table and the minerals contained within create natural ground voids of dissolved rock.

230 - 65 million years ago

Dinosaurs.

Skipping over a bit...

15,000 years ago

Palaeolithic settlers first inhabit the hills, leaving behind an assortment of sharp stone implements. Several "cave paintings"/engravings from this era can be found, both above and below ground.

10,000 years ago

Roughly believed to be when the potential pits were first reached by humans, coincides with the first evidence of monogastric creatures in artwork. Underground inhabitation abruptly stops at some point during this period.

5000 years ago

Latest evidence of underground inhabitation before the overground is exclusively inhabited. First evidence of neolithic farmers clearing the great forests that once covered the hills. Still evidence of underground burial tombs within the caves, along with stone monoliths and circles above ground.

3000 years ago

Generally regarded as the period where our town was established. Technological advancements and flourishing industry herald an age of progress.

2000 years ago

In the year 568 BC 'Rus Runk' a local legend, supposedly, drove back a "great and ugly beast with multiple vicious poison spitting heads", the beast attacked as Rus travelled into town early one morning and he was only able to drive the beast back with help from his work horse - Likely the first human encounter with the Leg Lurker.

1000 years ago (1098 AC)

First documented sightings of the monogastric creatures, originally referred to as 'face-eaters'. Potential genesis of the traditional flesh eating figures that have haunted peoples dreams ever since...

1742

RMPR (Registry for Missing Persons Report) established after 2000 missing person cases (since records starting in 1500) goes unsolved.

1868

First documented capture of a "monogastric entity" found sneaking around a barn on the edge of town. First documented use of the term "monogastric" after the specimen was cut open and what they found was lackluster. Sightings increase in number from this point on, as do disappearances.

1897

Monogastric population explodes, recorded sightings exceed 100 and the monogastric creatures enter the public consciousness as a permanent nuisance, venturing out into the hills no longer deemed safe.

1903

First official survey of the hills conducted, a crew of 14 set out. Only 4 return.


Some assorted sightings:

1912

The village times reported a sighting of "a fish or whatever it was".

1932

Miss K Dillinger reported seeing a "long limbed man" stalking through the treeline just north of town.

1956

George Henrys told the village council he'd "been scared 2/5ths to death!" by a small creature described as "a human head on legs" that fled his outhouse early one morning.


1992

Gizmo Dyer and his posse take up arms against the Leg Lurker, driving it underground. Thus begins the manflesh reign of terror.

1997

Gizmo Dyer leads his famous expedition deep underground, the first in hundreds of years, where his is able to find the MOAORG

2000

MMMOM established and monogastric creatures begin to be driven down underground or mulched up at a staggering rate.

2004

I part ways with the MMC

June 28th 2005

Incident at MMC-Skunkworks results in the disastrous loss of 32 lives and escape of the ORGs along with the GWDD and potentially other dangerous things. GD falls ill and dies shortly after.

December 28th 2008

The hills officially declared safe as all (most) monogastric creatures are reported to have "either been mulched up or driven down".

January 1st 2009

MMC officially dissolved

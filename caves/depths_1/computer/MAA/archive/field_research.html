'Face-Eater' Documentation

Denotes assorted collection of documents/artwork talking about 'face-eaters', creatures without facial features. Some of the earliest documentation concerning the monogastric. 'face-eater' was later taken by certain monogastric entities and used when talking about multigastric humans.

Most documents are held in museum archives not available to the public photographs and scans can be found online though.

1868 Report

Sighting and capture of a creature dubbed 'monogastric'. Dissection undergone and diagrams/illustrations created demonstrating the enigmatic interior lives of these things.

1903 Hills Survey

Townsmen invited to take part in expedition into the hills to evaluate the growing monogastric threat. 4 of the 14 participants return without much to show for it other than some brief documentation and event logs.
